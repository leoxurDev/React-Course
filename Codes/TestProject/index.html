Adolf Hitler (1889–1945) was an Austrian-born German politician who led Nazi Party and ruled Germany from 1933 until his death in 1945. He established a totalitarian dictatorship known as the Third Reich. Hitler is most known for: * Starting World War II by invading Poland in 1939. * Orchestrating the Holocaust, the systematic murder of about six million Jews, along with millions of other victims including Romani people, disabled individuals, political opponents, Soviet prisoners of war, and others. * Promoting extremist ideology based on antisemitism, racism, ultranationalism, and authoritarianism. Key timeline: * 1889: Born in Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary. * 1914–1918: Served in the German Army during World War I. * 1923: Led the failed Beer Hall Putsch in Munich. * 1933: Became Chancellor of Germany. * 1934: Consolidated power as Führer. * 1939: Invaded Poland, beginning World War II. * 1945: Died by suicide in Berlin as Soviet forces closed in. Hitler’s rule caused massive destruction across Europe and is widely regarded as one of the darkest periods in modern history. His ideology and actions are condemned worldwide.
